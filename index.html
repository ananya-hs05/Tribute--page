Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man of India," was an aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him a revered place in the country's history.

Timeline of His Life

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1960: Graduated from Madras Institute of Technology as an aeronautical engineer.
  • 1980: Played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3.
  • 1998: Led the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India.
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
